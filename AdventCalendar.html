Prologue: Second Coming Incoming

Liturgical Year 101

The Advent season does not begin with the baby in the manger, but with the King returning in glory. The church calendar resets here, looking forward to the End of Time before looking back to Bethlehem.

1st Sunday of Advent

"Liturgical Year 101: Second Coming Incoming!"

Matt 24:37-44 / Mark 13:33-37 / Luke 21:25-28, 34-36

The title reflects the urgency of the Gospel readings across all three cycles (A, B, C). Whether it is the days of Noah or the command to "stay awake," the theme is alert preparation for the Parousia (the Second Coming). It is "Liturgical Year 101" because this is the foundational orientation of the Christian life: waiting for the Master's return.

Part 1: A True Hope

From 1st Monday to 2nd Wednesday

The first phase of Advent focuses on the fulfillment of Isaiah's prophecies through the ministry of Jesus. We see the Messiah who heals, feeds, and gathers.

1st Monday

"Say and Be Healed: Faith of a Centurion"

Matthew 8:5-11

The Centurion understands that Jesus' authority transcends space. He doesn't need to be physically present; He only needs to "Say." This defines Advent faith: trusting the Word of the Lord before we see Him.

1st Tuesday

"You'll See what Other Wanted"

Luke 10:21-24

Jesus rejoices in the Holy Spirit. The "Others" are the prophets and kings of the Old Testament who longed to see what the disciples see. We are the beneficiaries of that ancient hope.

1st Wednesday

"A Meal for 4000 Gentiles"

Matthew 15:29-37

The feeding of the 4,000 (distinct from the 5,000) typically symbolizes the inclusion of the Gentiles. The Messiah provides a banquet not just for Israel, but for the nations.

1st Thursday

"Don't Just Say 'Lord, Lord', But Do Like a Builder on a Rock"

Matthew 7:21, 24-27

Advent waiting is active, not passive. To wait correctly is to build a foundation on the Rock of obedience, rather than the sand of empty vocal praise.

1st Friday

"When Jesus Open the Two Blind Men's Eyes"

Matthew 9:27-31

The central metaphor of Advent: Darkness into Light. The blind men cry out "Son of David," recognizing the Messiah by faith before they see Him with eyes.

1st Saturday

"Mission: To Israelites"

Matthew 9:35 - 10:1, 5a, 6-8

Before the Great Commission, there was the specific mission to the "lost sheep of the house of Israel." Jesus fulfills the specific promises to the Jews first.

2nd Sunday - INTERLUDE

John Baptist's Ministry

Matthew 3:1-12 / Mark 1:1-8 / Luke 3:1-6

The narrative shifts gears here. We move from Isaiah's ancient prophecies to the immediate Precursor. John the Baptist enters the scene, bridging the gap between the Old Testament and the New.

2nd Monday

"Healing is Forgiveness: When Jesus Healed a Paralytic"

Luke 5:17-26

The Pharisees asked, "Who can forgive sins but God alone?" Jesus demonstrates that the visible miracle (walking) validates the invisible one (forgiveness).

2nd Tuesday

"A Lost Sheep Is Found"

Matthew 18:12-14

God is not a passive waiter; He is an active searcher. This title highlights the tender side of judgment—the Shepherd seeking the one wanderer to bring them home for Christmas.

2nd Wednesday

"Jesus: A Heart of Gold"

Matthew 11:28-30

"Come to me, all who labor." The title "Heart of Gold" captures the essence of the Sacred Heart—the gentle and humble refuge for those exhausted by the world.

Part 2: Advent and John Baptist

Focusing on the Precursor (2nd Thu - Dec 16)

The middle of Advent belongs to the Voice Crying in the Wilderness. The readings focus on the relationship between Jesus and John, and the crisis of authority surrounding John's baptism as we approach the transition to the O Antiphons.

2nd Thursday

"Jesus on John Baptist (Part 2): 'A Greater Than a Prophet Is Here!'"

Matthew 11:11-15

Jesus identifies John as the hinge of history—the greatest figure of the old era, yet standing on the threshold of the new Kingdom.

2nd Friday

"Jesus on John Baptist (Part 3): When Others Heckled John as If He Had a Demon..."

Matthew 11:16-19

A study in rejection. The generation is satisfied by neither John's fasting ("he has a demon") nor Jesus' feasting ("a glutton"). Wisdom is vindicated only by her children.

2nd Saturday

"A Question after the Transfiguration: John was Elijah whom Others Persecuted!"

Matthew 17:9a, 10-13

Descending from the Mount of Transfiguration, Jesus solves the scribal riddle: Elijah has come in the person of John, and his suffering prefigures the Cross.

3rd Sunday: Gaudete (Joy)

"Find Joy through John Baptist"

Year A

"Jesus on John Baptist (Part 1): 'A Greater Than a Prophet Is Here!'"

Matt 11:2-11
Year B

"John's Ministry from John Evangelist's Perspective"

John 1:6-8, 19-28
Year C

"John's Dialogues"

Luke 3:10-18

VAR The Variable Days (Dec 12-16)

These readings occur on specific weekdays falling between the 3rd Sunday and Dec 17.

3rd Monday

Dec 12-16

"Authority Questioned! Where Did John's Baptism Come From?"

Matthew 21:23-27

The chief priests are trapped by their own political maneuvering, unable to accept John's authority without condemning themselves.

3rd Tuesday

Dec 13-16

"Parable of the Two Sons: When Sinners Are Baptized by John while Pharisees Are Stubborn"

Matthew 21:28-32

A stark contrast between the "righteous" who say yes but do nothing, and the "sinners" who say no but eventually repent and believe John.

Ember Wednesday

Dec 14-16

"John: 'Blessed Is He, Whosoever Shall Not Be Offended in Me!'"

Luke 7:18b-23

Jesus reassures the imprisoned Baptist. The Messiah isn't a military conqueror, but a healer of the broken. Blessed are those who don't stumble over this humble reality.

3rd Thursday

Dec 15-16

"Comments on John: Mission of the Forerunner"

Luke 7:24-30

Jesus praises John's rugged mission. He was not a reed shaken by the wind, but a prophet and more than a prophet.

Ember Friday

Only Dec 16

"When John's Witnessing Words Gave Way to the Lord's Greater Witnessing Word!"

John 5:33-36

The final word on John before the O Antiphons. He was the burning and shining lamp, but now the True Light has arrived. The works of Jesus now testify louder than the voice of John.

Ember Saturday

Before Dec 17

OMITTED

Because December 17th always falls in the O Antiphons period, the Ember Saturday reading is superseded by the O Sapientia reading (Genealogy).

Part 3: The O Antiphons & Pre-Christmas Stories

December 17 - 24

The "Octave before Christmas." The mood shifts to intense anticipation. The ancient "O Antiphons" (O Sapientia, O Adonai, etc.) mark these days. Below is the guide for both standard weekdays and the complex variations when the 4th Sunday of Advent intervenes.

Part 4: Significant Feasts

Saints & Solemnities in the Season

Advent is not just a season of waiting; it is punctuated by great feasts that highlight the missionary and Marian dimensions of the Incarnation, with special resonance for the Church in Vietnam.

Nov 30

St. Andrew, Apostle

Matthew 4:18-22

Rank: Feast (Universal)

Andrew is the Protoclete (First-Called). His feast is the "hinge" of the liturgical year. Depending on the day of the week, the title shifts significantly:

If Thu/Fri of OT 34 "Andrew's Call on Last Week"
If Sat of OT 34 (Last Day) "Last Catch of the Church Year"
If 1st Sunday of Advent OMITTED
If Mon-Wed after 1st Sunday "First Call on New Church Year: 'I Will Make Y'all Fishers of Men'"
Dec 03

St. Francis Xavier

Mark 16:15-20

"Go and Preach to all Nations"

The "Great Commission" reading aligns with Xavier's role as the Patron of Missions. He lived this Gospel literally, traveling to the ends of the earth.

Rank & Status

Proper Feast in Vietnam; Obligatory Memorial elsewhere.

Rubric Exception

OMITTED if December 3 falls on the 1st Sunday of Advent.

Dec 08

The Immaculate Conception

Luke 1:26-38

"A 'Fiat' from the Immaculate Mom"

Solemnity (Universal). Patroness of Ho Chi Minh City. The Gospel recounts the Annunciation—the moment the Immaculate Conception bore fruit in the Incarnation.

ℹ️
Transfer Rule: If December 8 falls on the 2nd Sunday of Advent, this Solemnity is transferred to Monday, December 9.
Dec 09

Dedication of Notre-Dame Basilica of Saigon

John 4:19-21

"Worship in Truth"

Historical Note: The Basilica was dedicated on December 8, 1959. However, because Dec 8 is the Solemnity of the Immaculate Conception, this Proper Feast for the Archdiocese of Saigon is celebrated on December 9.

"Woman, believe me, the hour is coming when neither on this mountain nor in Jerusalem will you worship the Father."

The Reading: This Gospel is from the Commons of the Dedication of a Church. It is the same reading used for any church dedication elsewhere (where such a dedication is ranked as a Solemnity for that parish). It reminds us that while we honor the physical stone of the Basilica, the true Temple is the believer worshipping in Spirit and Truth.

Lectionary Notes

Official Rubrics

Advent Sundays

"Each Gospel reading has a distinctive theme: the Lord's coming at the end of time (First Sunday of Advent), John the Baptist (Second and Third Sunday), and the events that prepared immediately for the Lord's birth (Fourth Sunday). The Old Testament readings are prophecies about the Messiah and the Messianic age, especially from the Book of Isaiah. The readings from an Apostle contain exhortations and proclamations, in keeping with the different themes of Advent."

(Lectionary for Mass, "Introduction," chap. 5, par. 93)

Advent Weekdays

"There are two series of readings: one to be used from the beginning of Advent until 16 December; the other from 17 to 24 December. In the first part of Advent there are readings from the Book of Isaiah, distributed in accord with the sequence of the book itself and including the more important texts that are also read on the Sundays. For the choice of the weekday Gospel the first reading has been taken into consideration. On Thursday of the second week the readings from the Gospel concerning John the Baptist begin. The first reading is either a continuation of Isaiah or a text chosen in view of the Gospel. In the last week before Christmas the events that immediately prepared for the Lord's birth are presented from the Gospel of Matthew (chapter 1) and Luke (chapter 1). The texts in the first reading, chosen in view of the Gospel reading, are from different Old Testament books and include important Messianic prophecies."

(Lectionary for Mass, "Introduction," chap. 5, par. 94)
